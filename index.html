How to Start Your Own Film

This guide contains the first part of creating a film. For additional guidance on the entire filmmaking process, visit the wikiHow page at the bottom of this guide.
Can take anywhere from a month to a year
Awesome rating: 80% awesome

Do you want to make your own film but don't know where to start? Follow this step-by-step process to help guide you on where to start! Each step is broken down into manageable pieces to ensure you create a fun experience and successful start to your film!

  • What You'll Need:

  • Paper or a text software
  • Drawing supplies or illustration software
  • Actors or volunteers
  • A crew
  • Purchased or rented filming equipment
  • Lots of determination!

PART 1: Preparation

3 Steps (plus a video)

  1. Brainstorm your film

    It is important to have a good idea about what your film is going to be about as well as how it will look like. For example, do you want to convey a story or document something? Will it be animated or use the environment around you? What will the tone be (serious, funny, thriller)? If you need help coming up with ideas, look back at films that inspired you to create your own or talk to people to gain new ideas.

  2. How To Brainstorm Ideas For A Short Film - D4Darious

    If you're still stuck on how to brainstorm ideas for your film, check out this video explaing different brainstorming methods that can help you come up with great film ideas!

  3. Begin writing your script

    This is where you start finalizing the key details from the previous step in order to create a script of your film. Here you will include actor's lines and scenes. If your film will be animated, include voice actor lines and detail in writing the kind of images that will appear throughout your film in sequential order.

    If you can't write a script, you can search online for scripts that others have already written. Make sure to get the author's permission to use their script.

  4. Draw a storyboard

    A storyboard depics the scenes, camera angles, and camera and character movement. These are especially helpful if you're animating your film. Keep in mind that a storyboard does not have to be detailed.

    Quick sketches like stickfigures and a small caption are more than enough to help guide your film.

    5. PART 2: Preproduction

    4 Steps

    1. Look for locations

      If your film will be shot live, look for locations around your area that can be used as scenery for your film. This can be inside your own place or outdoors.

      Make sure you get permission to film outdoors if you are shooting on private property.

    2. Get actors

      Whether your film is shot in real life or will be voiced and animated, you must aquire someone to play these parts. These people can be your friends, family, strangers, or even yourself.

      Be sure to stay safe when working with strangers, as well as credit the people who contruted to your film.

    3. Recruit a crew

      Though it isn't impossible to create a film on your own, it never hurts to have a team that can help you with the cinematography, lighting, editing, and overall any other manual labor. These people can be your friends or people that you know who are also interested in fimmaking.

      Strive to have a crew that will work well together in order to be more successful and efficient in the production of your film.

    4. Aquire filming equipment

      Your film will determine what kind of equipment you will need. If you're animating it, you get to decide if you want to do so by drawing by hand, use a drawing tablet, or any other medium. If you're filming in real life, you will need cameras, lights, and microphones.

      You can rent or purchase this equipment.
